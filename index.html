Information on Salt
Salt is crucial for life; however, Australians are consuming to much of it.
Salt is commonly known as salt and its scientific name is sodium chloride (NaCI). 
It is the sodium in sodium chloride that is harmful when consumed in large amounts.
Consuming too much salt over a long period of time can cause your blood pressure to increase which results in higher risk of high blood pressure.
High blood pressure is a major risk factor for heart disease. 

How Much Salt is too Much?
An average Australian consumes nearly double of the amount of sodium needed for good health.
Adult bodies (14 or older) only require 1 to 2 grams of salt (460-920 mg of sodium) daily to function normally.
The recommended maximum salt consumption for adults is 5 grams of salt per day (2000 mg of sodium).
